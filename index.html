Dash Cam Buying Guide

If you're new to dash cams or unsure what features matter, here's what we prioritized in our analysis:

Video Quality

Resolution matters, but it's not everything. A 1080p camera with a good sensor (like Sony's IMX series) will often outperform a 4K camera with a cheap sensor, especially in low light. That said, 1080p is the minimum we'd recommend‚Äîanything less and you'll struggle to read license plates in footage.

Night Performance

Most accidents happen in low-light conditions. Look for cameras with Sony STARVIS or similar low-light sensors. The Vantrue E1 Pro excels here, but even our budget pick performs adequately after dark.

Reliability Over Features

A dash cam that crashes, overheats, or corrupts footage is worthless. All three of our picks have proven track records across thousands of user reviews. We specifically avoided cameras with high failure rates, even if their specs looked impressive.

GPS: Nice to Have

GPS logs your speed and location, which can be valuable for insurance claims or legal disputes. Two of our three picks include it built-in. If you go with the 70mai, the GPS module add-on is about $25.

Installation

All our picks use a simple 12V adapter (cigarette lighter) for power. For a cleaner look, you can hardwire them using a kit ($15-30). Hardwiring also enables parking mode, which records when the car is off and detects impacts.

Don't Forget the SD Card

Most dash cams don't include storage. We recommend a high-endurance microSD card (not a regular one) rated for continuous recording. Samsung PRO Endurance or SanDisk High Endurance are solid choices. Get at least 64GB‚Äî128GB is better for 4K cameras.

What We Didn't Prioritize

We skipped features that add cost without adding safety value:

  • Cloud storage subscriptions ‚Äî unnecessary recurring cost for most users
  • Built-in screens larger than 3" ‚Äî you're not watching movies on it
  • Driver fatigue detection ‚Äî gimmicky and often inaccurate
  • Speed camera alerts ‚Äî use Waze or Google Maps instead
